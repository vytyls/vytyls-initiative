# Reality Fold Notation (RFN): A Formal Methodology for the Systematic Mapping of Subjective Experience

**Abstract:** The study of subjective and anomalous experience has long been hampered by a lack of rigorous, systematic methods for data collection and analysis. Traditional narrative accounts are rich but difficult to compare, while quantitative measures often flatten the phenomena they seek to describe. In this paper, we introduce a complete, two-part methodology designed to bridge this gap. We first present the **Modal Layer Field Map (MLFM)**, a six-layer topological framework for categorizing distinct strata of perception, from the somatic to the extra-perceptual. We then detail **Reality Fold Notation (RFN)**, a comprehensive symbolic language used to identify, categorize, and track events—such as synchronicities, symbolic resonances, and causal anomalies—that occur on this map. Finally, we present the **Field Case Log (FCL)** and the **"Comb" Protocol**, structured data collection instruments that operationalizes this framework, allowing for the translation of qualitative experiences into a queryable, analyzable dataset. We argue that this integrated system offers a new, robust, and falsifiable methodology for the scientific and philosophical inquiry of consciousness.
