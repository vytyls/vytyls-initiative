**The Core Mathematical Operators**
RFN uses a intentionally minimal set of mathematical operators to document shifts and interactions across the six perceptual strata of the **Modal Layer Field Map (MLFM)**. The primary operators include:
*   **$\Phi$ (The Field Operator):** The universal symbol for the global FoC acting upon a local system, representing the background potential or "Global Intention" of the lattice. 
*   **$\triangleright$ (The Interaction Operator):** A directional operator indicating "Actuates," "Informs," or "Couples with." It replaces a standard equals sign to emphasize the *active* nature of a coupling (e.g., $Intent \triangleright Manifestation$).
*   **$\sigma$ (The Symbolic Density Function):** A function that measures the concentration of archetypal or metaphorical content within a specific event or modal layer.
*   **$\tau$ (The Temporal Binding Function):** Represents the binding of an event to linear time (chronos), typically denoting Cognitive or Somatic sequences where events are experienced causally.
*   **$\Delta$ (The Difference Operator):** Indicates a shift, disruption, or delta in the standard field state, often marking physical anomalies or "glitches" that deviate from expected baseline physics.
*   **$F_t$ (The Time-Fold Operator):** Maps an event across a non-linear path from time $t$ to $t+\delta t$. This operator mathematically describes "The Corkscrew" or recursive temporal loops, capturing instances where a layer intersects its own time-folded future (like déjà vu).
*   **$AI$ (Active Information):** Adapted from the physicist David Bohm, this operator represents information with the capacity to *in-form* (put form into) energy, acting as the "carrier wave" of meaning moving through layers.
